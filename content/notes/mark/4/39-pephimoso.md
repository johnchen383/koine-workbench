title: πεφίμωσο — a perfect imperative
tags: perfect, imperative, rare-form
---
Jesus rebukes the sea: **σιώπα, πεφίμωσο** — "Be silent! Be muzzled!" The second verb is a **perfect passive imperative**, one of the rarest forms in the NT (the perfect imperative occurs only here and Acts 15:29).

The perfect's stative force makes the command something like *"be muzzled — and stay that way"*: a state to be entered and maintained. English can only gesture at it ("Be still!" in most versions flattens both the muzzling metaphor and the aspect). A study translation might risk *"Silence! Stay muzzled!"*

φιμόω is also the word used for muzzling an ox (1 Corinthians 9:9) and for Jesus "silencing" demons (Mark 1:25 — same verb, aorist there). The echo suggests Mark presents the storm-calming like an exorcism.
