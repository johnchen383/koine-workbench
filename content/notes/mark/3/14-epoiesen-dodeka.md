---
verse: 14
category: lexical
title: ἐποίησεν δώδεκα — he "made" twelve?
tags: lexical-range
---
Literally Jesus **"made"** (ἐποίησεν) twelve. English versions say "appointed," "ordained" (KJV), or "chose." ποιέω has a broad range, and "appoint/institute" is well attested (cf. LXX 1 Samuel 12:6, "the LORD who *made* Moses and Aaron" = installed them in office; Hebrews 3:2).

The lesson: a lemma's **gloss is not its meaning** — meaning is selected by context from the word's attested range. Some also hear an echo of creation language (Mark says he made twelve "*that they might be with him*"), but don't over-read: "appointed" is doing normal Greek work here.

Note the textual wrinkle: some early manuscripts add "whom he also named apostles" (probably harmonized from Luke 6:13); compare your English versions.
