verse: 21
category: translation
title: οἱ παρ᾽ αὐτοῦ — "his family" went out to seize him?
tags: idiom, ambiguity
---
Literally "**those from beside him**" — an idiom whose referent must be inferred: his relatives/family (most likely, cf. v. 31 where his mother and brothers arrive), or more loosely his associates/friends.

And what did they say? ἔλεγον ὅτι **ἐξέστη** — "he is out of his mind / beside himself." Two decisions stack up:

1. **Who**: "his family" (most modern versions) vs "his friends" (KJV) — bolder vs safer.
2. **Who said it**: ἔλεγον could be "they [the family] were saying" or impersonal, "people were saying" — some translators soften the scandal of Jesus' family thinking him mad by choosing the impersonal reading.

A verse where lexicon, grammar, and theological discomfort visibly pull on the translation — compare your versions and watch them squirm.
