title: πεπλήρωται and ἤγγικεν — perfects with present force
tags: perfect, eschatology
---
Jesus' programmatic announcement uses two perfects: **πεπλήρωται** ὁ καιρός ("the time *has been fulfilled* — and now stands full") and **ἤγγικεν** ἡ βασιλεία ("the kingdom *has drawn near*").

The perfect asserts a completed event whose *result now holds*. That is exactly the theological freight here — the decisive moment has arrived and its effects are present.

ἤγγικεν is famously contested: does "has come near" mean *arrived* (C. H. Dodd's "realized eschatology": translate "has come") or *approached, is at hand* (imminent but not yet)? Compare the versions: KJV/ASV "is at hand," many modern versions "has come near." Your rendering of one Greek verb takes a position in a century-old scholarly debate — a vivid case of why translation justification matters.
