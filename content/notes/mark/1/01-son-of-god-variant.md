title: Is "Son of God" original?
tags: textual-criticism
---
υἱοῦ θεοῦ ("Son of God") is missing from some important early witnesses (notably the original hand of Codex Sinaiticus א*), and scholars are divided:

- **For omission being original**: the shorter reading is generally preferred; a scribe expanding a sacred title is more likely than one dropping it.
- **For inclusion being original**: the string of six genitives ending in nomina sacra abbreviations (ΙΥ ΧΥ ΥΥ ΘΥ) makes an accidental skip of the eye very easy; and "Son of God" frames Mark's whole narrative (1:11; 9:7; 15:39).

Most modern editions print it, some in brackets. A translation note ("some manuscripts omit *Son of God*") is the honest rendering of the uncertainty. This is a good first example of how the *text being translated* is itself a decision prior to translation.
