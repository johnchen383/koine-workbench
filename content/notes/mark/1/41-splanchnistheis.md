title: Compassion or anger? σπλαγχνισθείς vs ὀργισθείς
tags: textual-criticism, famous-variant
---
Most manuscripts read **σπλαγχνισθείς** ("moved with compassion"), but Codex Bezae (D) and some Old Latin witnesses read **ὀργισθείς** ("moved with anger") — and many text critics take the harder reading seriously: why would a scribe change "compassion" *to* "anger," while softening "anger" to "compassion" is easy to imagine? (Note Mark 3:5 has no problem attributing anger to Jesus, and Matthew/Luke both drop the emotion word entirely — as if their copy of Mark had something awkward.)

- NIV (2011) actually translates "Jesus was **indignant**."
- Most versions stay with "moved with compassion/pity."

Whichever you print, this verse is the standard classroom example of the text-critical principle *lectio difficilior potior* ("the harder reading is stronger") — and of its limits, since the external evidence for σπλαγχνισθείς is overwhelming.
