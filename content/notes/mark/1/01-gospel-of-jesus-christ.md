title: Whose gospel? The genitive Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ
tags: genitive, ambiguity
---
τοῦ εὐαγγελίου **Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ** — "the gospel *of Jesus Christ*." The genitive is genuinely ambiguous, and the ambiguity matters:

- **Objective genitive**: the gospel *about* Jesus Christ — Jesus is the content of the message. Fits Mark's book, which is precisely the story of Jesus.
- **Subjective genitive**: the gospel *proclaimed by* Jesus Christ — Jesus is the messenger. Fits 1:14–15, where Jesus himself preaches the gospel.

Most translations keep the ambiguous English "of," which is a legitimate translation *decision*: preserve the ambiguity rather than resolve it. Some commentators argue Mark intends both (a "plenary" genitive). If your translation must choose, note what you gain and lose either way.
