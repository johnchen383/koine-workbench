title: "In Isaiah the prophet" — but the quote is composite
tags: ot-quotation, textual-criticism
---
Mark attributes the quotation to **Isaiah**, but vv. 2–3 splice together three texts: Exodus 23:20 ("I send my messenger before your face"), Malachi 3:1 ("who will prepare your way"), and only then Isaiah 40:3 ("a voice crying in the wilderness…").

Two consequences for the translator:

1. **Textual variant**: the Byzantine text (behind the KJV) reads "in the **prophets**" — almost certainly a scribal smoothing of the difficulty. Compare KJV against ASV/WEB on this verse and you are looking at a text-critical decision, not a translation one.
2. **Don't fix it in translation**: rendering it "in the prophets" or footnoting it away removes a deliberate Markan move — naming Isaiah flags the *dominant* text of the composite and signals that the whole Gospel unfolds under Isaiah's "way of the LORD."

Note also ὁδός ("way") introduced here becomes a Markan keyword (8:27; 9:33–34; 10:32, 52).
