verse: 15
category: grammar
title: μετανοεῖτε καὶ πιστεύετε — present imperatives
tags: imperative, aspect
---
Both commands are **present** imperatives, carrying imperfective (ongoing) aspect: not "make a one-off decision" but *"repent and keep believing"* — a summons to a continuing stance. English imperatives can't show this without paraphrase; most translations leave it invisible.

Also note πιστεύετε **ἐν** τῷ εὐαγγελίῳ: πιστεύω with ἐν is rare (classical Greek uses the dative or εἰς). It may reflect Semitic idiom (Hebrew הֶאֱמִין בְּ). "Believe **in** the gospel" is fine English, but be aware you are translating an unusual construction, not the ordinary one.
