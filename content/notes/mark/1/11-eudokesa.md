title: εὐδόκησα — an aorist rendered as English present
tags: aorist, aspect
---
The voice from heaven ends ἐν σοὶ **εὐδόκησα** — aorist, literally "I *was pleased* with you." Yet nearly every English version says "I **am** well pleased." Why?

The aorist here is best taken as **constative/timeless**: it sums up the Father's pleasure as a whole fact, without locating it at a past moment (some grammars compare the Hebrew stative perfect standing behind the idiom, cf. Isaiah 42:1). English simple past ("I was pleased") would wrongly imply the pleasure has ended.

This is a clean example of the golden rule of tense translation: **Greek tense encodes aspect; time comes from context.** Translating aorist mechanically as English past is the single most common beginner error.
