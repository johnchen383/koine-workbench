title: εἰς ἄφεσιν ἁμαρτιῶν — the force of εἰς
tags: prepositions, theology
---
John proclaims "a baptism of repentance **εἰς** forgiveness of sins." The preposition εἰς with the accusative usually marks direction or goal, so the standard rendering is purposive: *"for / unto / leading to* the forgiveness of sins."

Watch what each option asserts:

- **"for the forgiveness of sins"** — forgiveness is the goal of the repentance-baptism (most translations).
- **"because of forgiveness"** — a causal εἰς has been proposed (mostly in debates about baptism's efficacy), but causal εἰς is doubtful Greek and special pleading here.

Also note the genitive chain: βάπτισμα **μετανοίας** is a "baptism *characterized by* repentance" (attributed/descriptive genitive) — not a baptism that produces repentance. English "a repentance-baptism" is ugly but shows the structure.
