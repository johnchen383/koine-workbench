title: οἱ υἱοὶ τοῦ νυμφῶνος — a Semitic idiom
tags: idiom, semitism
---
Literally "the **sons of the bridechamber**" — a Semitic idiom (בְּנֵי + noun) meaning *people belonging to / characterized by* something: here, **the wedding guests** (or specifically the groom's attendants).

The translator's spectrum:

- **KJV**: "children of the bridechamber" — formal equivalence, preserves the idiom, opaque to modern readers.
- **Modern versions**: "the wedding guests" / "the friends of the bridegroom" — dynamic equivalence, communicates the referent, loses the Hebraic texture.

This is the classic formal-vs-functional trade-off in miniature. Similar "sons of X" idioms recur throughout the NT ("sons of thunder" 3:17, "sons of light," "son of perdition") — decide on a consistent policy.
