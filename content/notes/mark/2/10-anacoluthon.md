title: Broken syntax — who is Jesus talking to mid-verse?
tags: anacoluthon, discourse
---
The sentence starts addressed to the scribes ("but so that you may know [ἵνα δὲ εἰδῆτε] that the Son of Man has authority…") and then breaks off — **λέγει τῷ παραλυτικῷ**, "he says to the paralytic: 'Rise…'". The ἵνα clause never gets its main verb; the narrative supplies it by *doing* it.

This is an **anacoluthon** (broken construction). Translators handle it with punctuation: most insert a dash or parenthesis — "…authority on earth to forgive sins" — *he said to the paralytic* — "I say to you, rise…". Whatever you print, don't smooth the break away entirely; the interrupted syntax is part of the drama. (Note also λέγει — a historical present at the hinge of the action.)
